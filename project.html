School Feeding

Providing breakfast and lunch to vulnerable students, improving health, focus, and academic performance.

Ethiopia has a significant need for school feeding programs to address widespread hunger and malnutrition among schoolchildren, particularly those from low-income families, which can impact their health, education, and development. School feeding programs are seen as a crucial component of a holistic approach to improving child well-being and promoting social equity. Additionally, Ethiopian government has recently initiated the school feeding program at primary schools only. The need for the feeding program for secondary public schools particularly in Addis Ababa is very high since it is an overlooked area in which students coming from low income families have not get meals in order to support their education. NGOs are not also focused on supporting these secondary schools in the capital but dedicated their support at other regions. Hence, the need to expand the program to other public secondary schools across the capital as well as initiating the feeding program has become all time high.

Income Generation Activities

Empowering vulnerable mothers with startup capital, skills training, and self-help groups for sustainability.

Creating an income generation project for vulnerable mothers can empower them economically and socially. Our objective is to provide vulnerable mothers with skills, resources, and opportunities to generate sustainable income. The target group includes single mothers, low-income families, and those facing socio economic challenges at Woreda 07 Yeka Sub City. The selection has been conducted jointly with Yeka Sub Cities Woreda 07 Women and Children Office. We have conducted mini assessments and focus group discussions to identify the specific needs, skills, and interests of the target group. We have also assessed local market demands to align the project with viable income-generating activities. We have also conducted skills training by Woreda Job and Skills Expert: Offer workshops in various areas such as: • Spice Production and Selling • Food production • Financial literacy (budgeting, saving) We have provided materials and tools needed for the chosen income-generating activities. Each mother has received a total of 10,000 ETB for start up and running capitals. The IGA group has also established an SHG group called Hibret and started to save with the minimum set amount. This income generation project aims not only to provide financial independence but also to build confidence and community among vulnerable mothers. By equipping them with skills and resources, we can foster resilience and improve their overall quality of life.

Socio-economic Support

Supporting outstanding vulnerable students with financial aid and mental wellness programs.

Eneho Fikir has been implementing a project at 13 targeted secondary schools to improve student outcomes. The project delivers encouraging results, particularly in the teachers training, students training, exposure visits, advocacy weeks on mental wellness, and goal setting for high school students. In addition, the project encourages best preforming students through supporting them with supplementary books and certificate of appreciation. On the other hand, among the best performing students, there are vulnerable students who are currently pursuing their education under difficult circumstances. The vulnerable students are also comes from low income families and displaced from other regions due to the ongoing conflicts. Accordingly, we have conducted a preliminary assessment on the situation of vulnerable students to support them based on their need and the school administration has also provided recommendations to assist those who are under difficult positions to continue their education. The targeted schools serve a diverse student population with a significant percentage coming from low-income families. The school has identified challenges in supporting students facing such as food insecurity, lack of scholastic materials, housing instability, trauma, and lack of access to healthcare, etc.

Sanitary Pad Support

Reducing absenteeism among female students by providing sanitary pads for better school participation.

Eneho Fikire has a strong ambition in supporting vulnerable girls to improve their education outcomes. For its realisation, we have been amplifying girl’s education to ensure girls at schools should receive the necessary support and excel in academic performance. We have identified 153 female students who are enrolled in to our feeding program at Tesfaberhan S.School. These female students were mentored by a teacher while they received their daily meals. This is to ensure their school attendance and better academic performances. Girls in schools have faced different challenges that can negatively influence their education. Among the challenges school girls face include lack of scholastic materials and dignity kits. Hence, we have provided sanitary pads to these girls so that they can properly continue their education. These girls who are also enrolled in to the school feeding program have been receiving sanitary pads as a means to continue their education that can potentially happen due to menstruation.

Humanitarian Support

Providing food, clothing, and essentials during crises for vulnerable families in Yeka Sub City.

Our Emergency Support Project is essential for providing immediate assistance to vulnerable populations during crises, such as natural disasters, health emergencies, or economic downturns. Our objective is to provide immediate relief and support to vulnerable individuals and families during emergencies, ensuring their basic needs are met and facilitating recovery. The target group includes low-income families, single parents, the elderly, individuals with disabilities, and other marginalized groups affected by emergencies which are residing at Yeka Sub City. We have collaborated with local authorities and organizations to gather data on the most urgent requirements (food, clothing, medical care, etc.). We have distributed essential items such as clothing, and other materials for needy target groups. The donations items were donated by ET Foundation and Individual collaborators who are willing to support. The Emergency Support Project aims to provide timely and effective assistance to those in need during crises. By addressing immediate needs while also laying the groundwork for recovery and resilience, this project can help vulnerable populations navigate challenging times and emerge stronger.

Empowering Disadvantaged High School Students in Addis Ababa Project with CPAR

Empowering disadvantaged high school students with career development and emotional support.

Eneho Fikir, in collaboration with CPAR, is successfully implementing a project aimed at commemorating the victims of ET 302 by empowering high school students in Addis Ababa, Ethiopia. The initiative focuses on 11 high schools selected from different sub cities within Addis Ababa. This project is initiated and funded by Transport Canada as one of the commemorative projects to make a meaningful impact in youth focused, community based, environmental and humanitarian perspectives. Eneho Fiker has launched the joint project with CPAR aimed at expanding its impact on the educational and social support for vulnerable students. Building upon the success of the first phase, this continuation emphasizes sustainable development through education, nutrition and skill building, various motivational as well as psycho social; support activities. The phase two continues to focus on holistic approach to enhance the project outcomes. Furthermore, On March 10, 2019, Ethiopian Airlines flight ET 302 crashed right after taking off from Addis Ababa Bole International Airport, near Addis Ababa Ethiopia claiming the lives of 157 people including 18 Canadians and many others with ties to Canada. On the fourth anniversary of the tragic accident, the ministry of transport announced that the government of Canada is providing financial support for commemorative project to honor and preserve the memory of the victims of the flight ET 302 in a long lasting visible and meaningful way. Hence, Canadian Physicians for Aid and Relief (CPAR) took the leading responsibility to work on disadvantaged high school students in Addis Ababa Ethiopia in areas of youth education, career development, social development sexual and reproductive health awareness, environmental awareness, and stewardship and humanitarian activities as well as several school gardening projects. In the implementation of the commemorative project, CPAR entered a sub grantee agreement with Eneho Fikir to carry out identified activities on motivational and social and career development endeavors in the selected 11 high schools in Addis Ababa Ethiopia. Eneho Fiker is granted a lead role in the implementation of the following activities per the sub grantee agreement reached with CPAR.

Medical Campaigns

Providing free medical services, including dental, eye, and cancer screenings, in partnership with health institutions.

Eneho Fikir in close collaboration with GIV Society has been organizing in-school medical campaigns for at least two times in a year. The campaign was conducted jointly with Paulos Hospital, Ethiopian Dental association and GIV Society at targeted schools. During the campaign volunteer Doctors form Paulos Hospital diagnosed and treated students, teachers and the surrounding community. Meantime, needy students and teachers received with eyeglasses and other referral medications. Furthermore, the medical campaigns provided a wide range of free medical services [dental, ophthalmology, dermatology, cervical cancer, etc.] for school children and the surrounding community. The event also helps school children for disease prevention through referral treatment and on spot treatment for minor medical cases. This kind of medical campaign has played its own role in improving education outcomes at the targeted school.
